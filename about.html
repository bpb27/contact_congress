contactingcongress@gmail.com

Each citizen should feel comfortable voicing their opinion to their elected representatives. We need to start participating in our democracy more than every two or four years, and contacting congress is one quick, easy, and impactful way to affect change. There are many sites that help you find contact information, but the goal of this site is to be comprehensive, quick, and easy to use.

Please help us keep this site accurate and up-to-date. If you encounter any mistakes or omissions, find any unlisted townhall schedules, or have an idea for the site, please let me know.

FAQs

What should I say when I call?

Here is a great article with some advice and guidance on how to have an impactful conversation with a congressional office.

Why do I get multiple reps for my zip code?

Reps sometimes share zip codes. Enter your address on our local reps page for more specificity.

Do they not have normal email addresses?

They no longer publicly list email. You have to send a message through their official website form. Calling is much more effective.

Can I use your data?

Yes. Here's the source. Let me know if you make anything cool.

Where did you get your data?

Each representative's official government page, and Wikipedia. Local rep info comes from Google's Civic Info API. ACA numbers come from the Kaiser Family Foundation.
